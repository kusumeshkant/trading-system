🔢 KPI Formulas (Plain Language)

Gross P&L

The raw profit/loss before any costs are deducted.

Long: Gross P&L = (Exit Price − Entry Price) × Quantity Short: Gross P&L = (Entry Price − Exit Price) × Quantity

Net P&L

The actual money you kept after paying fees and slippage.

Net P&L = Gross P&L − Fees Paid − Slippage

Win Rate

What percentage of your trades were profitable.

Win Rate = (Winning Trades ÷ Total Closed Trades) × 100

Profit Factor

How much you earn for every dollar you lose. A value above 1.0 means you're profitable overall.

Profit Factor = Total Gross Wins ÷ Total Gross Losses

Risk/Reward Ratio (R:R)

How much reward you target relative to your risk on each trade.

Long R:R = (Take Profit − Entry) ÷ (Entry − Stop Loss) Short R:R = (Entry − Take Profit) ÷ (Stop Loss − Entry)

Example: Entry 100, SL 95, TP 115 → R:R = (115−100)÷(100−95) = 15÷5 = 3.0 (displayed as 1:3)

Max Drawdown

The largest dollar drop from a peak in your equity curve. Measures your worst losing period.

Max Drawdown = Max(Peak Equity − Current Equity) across all trades

Sharpe Ratio

Risk-adjusted return. How much return you earn per unit of risk. Above 1.0 is good; above 2.0 is excellent.

Sharpe = (Avg Return − Risk Free Rate) ÷ StdDev(Returns) × √252

Trade Duration

Duration (minutes) = (Closed At − Opened At) in total minutes

💰 How P&L is Calculated

Step 1: Gross P&L

The raw profit before costs. For a Long trade:

Gross P&L = (Exit Price − Entry Price) × Quantity

Example: Bought 0.1 BTC at $60,000, sold at $62,000:

Gross P&L = ($62,000 − $60,000) × 0.1 = $2,000 × 0.1 = $200

Step 2: Net P&L

Subtract all costs from the gross profit:

Net P&L = Gross P&L − Fees − Slippage

Example: Gross $200, Fees $3, Slippage $0.50:

Net P&L = $200 − $3 − $0.50 = $196.50

P&L Percent

P&L% = (Gross P&L ÷ (Entry Price × Quantity)) × 100

↕️ Long vs Short Explained

Long Trade (Bullish)

You buy an asset expecting its price to rise. You profit when exit price > entry price.

  • Buy 1 BTC at $60,000 → Price rises to $65,000 → Profit: $5,000
  • Buy 1 BTC at $60,000 → Price falls to $55,000 → Loss: -$5,000

Short Trade (Bearish)

You sell an asset you don't own, expecting the price to fall. You profit when exit price < entry price.

  • Short 1 BTC at $60,000 → Price falls to $55,000 → Profit: $5,000
  • Short 1 BTC at $60,000 → Price rises to $65,000 → Loss: -$5,000

Stop Loss Direction

For a Long trade: Stop Loss must be below entry price.

For a Short trade: Stop Loss must be above entry price.

💸 Fees, Slippage, Quantity & R:R

Fees

The cost charged by your broker/exchange for executing a trade. On Binance spot, the standard fee is 0.1% of trade value per side. Enter the total fees (entry + exit combined).

Example: 0.1 BTC at $60,000 = $6,000 trade. Binance fee = 0.1% × 2 sides = $12 total fees.

Slippage

The difference between the price you expected and the price you actually got, in dollars. Market orders often have slippage; limit orders usually don't.

Example: Placed a market buy at $60,000 but filled at $60,050 for 0.1 BTC → Slippage = $5.

Quantity

The number of units of the asset you traded. For crypto, this is typically in base currency (e.g., BTC). For stocks, it's the number of shares.

  • Crypto: 0.01 BTC, 1.5 ETH, 100 SOL
  • Stocks: 10 shares of AAPL
  • Forex: 10000 units (0.1 lot)

Risk/Reward Ratio (R:R)

Measures how much you stand to gain relative to what you risk. A 1:2 R:R means for every $1 you risk, you target $2 profit.

Even with a 40% win rate, a 1:3 R:R is profitable:

4 wins × $3 = $12 profit 6 losses × $1 = $6 loss Net = +$6 on 10 trades

⚠️ Common Mistakes When Entering Trades

  • Exit Price for Long lower than Entry: On a Long trade, if Exit < Entry, the P&L will be negative. This is correct only if it was a losing trade.
  • Forgetting to set Closed At: If you fill in Exit Price but leave Closed At blank, duration won't be calculated.
  • Wrong Stop Loss direction: For a Long, SL must be below entry. For a Short, SL must be above entry. Entering wrong values makes R:R inaccurate.
  • Entering fees per side instead of total: Enter the combined fees (entry + exit). If each side costs $1, enter $2.
  • Wrong quantity: For crypto, enter the base asset quantity (BTC amount), not the USD value.
  • Not recording open trades: Log open positions too — leave Exit Price and Closed At blank until you close them.
  • Using wrong datetime format: The datetime field requires format: YYYY-MM-DD HH:MM. E.g. 2026-04-21 14:30.
